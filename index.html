01 The Problem

Most crypto trading analysis falls into one of two traps: single-indicator dependence (MVRV says buy, so buy) or narrative-driven positioning (ETF inflows = bullish, therefore long). Both approaches worked in previous cycles but have shown diminishing reliability in the post-ETF institutional era.

Since the January 2024 spot ETF approval, Bitcoin's market structure has fundamentally changed. On-chain metrics have decoupled from price action. Whale execution has fragmented across exchanges in 300-500 BTC micro-batches. Cycle indicators like Pi Cycle and 200W MA Heatmap failed to produce their historical overheat signals. The old playbook is broken.

This framework was built to address that structural shift — not by abandoning on-chain analysis, but by embedding it within a multi-layer decision architecture that adapts to the regime it operates in.

02 Architecture: Four Decision Layers

The model doesn't make one decision. It makes four, in sequence, each constraining the next. This mirrors how institutional commodity desks operate — you don't trade the spread without knowing the macro, and you don't size the position without knowing the spread.

Layer 1: Macro Regime Classification

Four macro variables — M2 money supply growth, DXY trend vs. 50-day SMA, VIX level, and High Yield spread change — are scored on a bull/bear axis. The net score classifies the current environment into one of four regimes, each with distinct position sizing rules.

EXPANSION
Liquidity expanding, dollar weakening, low volatility. Core long position with wide trailing stops. ~31% of historical days.
STABLE
Mixed signals, no clear direction. Moderate positioning with tighter risk controls. ~60% of historical days.
TIGHTENING
Liquidity contracting, dollar strengthening. Minimal or short exposure. ~5% of historical days.
BLACK SWAN
VIX spike, credit stress. Immediate risk reduction. ~4% of historical days.

Why this matters in 2025-2026: Last year's M2 expansion coincided with a BTC drawdown — a divergence that single-factor models couldn't explain. The multi-axis regime classification caught this: while M2 was expanding, DXY strengthened and VIX spiked intermittently, keeping the regime in STABLE rather than EXPANSION. The model correctly reduced position sizing during this period despite the "bullish" M2 narrative.

Layer 2: Market Phase

Within bull regimes, the model distinguishes between early-cycle accumulation (wide stops, patience through 20%+ corrections) and late-cycle distribution (tight stops, quick exits on MVRV overextension). This is directly inspired by commodity cycle management — you trade the front of a supercycle differently than the blow-off top.

Layer 3: Composite Score

Eight variables — MVRV Z-Score, NUPL, Fear & Greed, SOPR, VIX, DXY, 4-week and 12-week momentum — are combined with regime-conditional weights. In EXPANSION, on-chain valuation metrics dominate. In TIGHTENING, macro stress indicators take over. The score ranges 0-100, where high = fear (buy zone) and low = greed (sell zone). This contrarian framing proved 92% accurate at historical cycle inflection points.

Layer 4: Risk Management

Three independent risk mechanisms operate simultaneously: phase-specific trailing stops (20% in BULL_EARLY, 8% in BULL_LATE), a pre-crisis early warning system (VIX crossing 23 from below, or BTC below 200-day MA with 90-day drawdown exceeding 15%), and regime-based forced exits (BLACK_SWAN detection triggers immediate position reduction).

03 The Core Long Insight

The single most impactful discovery in this project: holding a large core position through bull regimes and letting the score provide only minor adjustments outperforms any score-driven entry/exit system.

Early versions (V11-V13) used the composite score to determine position size directly. Score 80 = 80% long, score 40 = 0%. This produced modest returns (+31%) with low drawdowns (-23%) but systematically missed the bulk of every rally. The model was too reactive — cutting positions on temporary fear spikes only to re-enter at higher prices.

The breakthrough came from inverting the logic: the regime determines the base position (68-98%), and the score provides ±8% micro-adjustments. This simple change — holding conviction through noise — took returns from +31% to +906% while maintaining directional accuracy. The commodity trader's instinct validated: in a structural bull move, the biggest risk is not being in the market.

"Signals big, stops late, re-entries rare."

06 Known Limitations & Active Development

In-Sample Backtest Bias
All results are in-sample. Walk-forward out-of-sample R² remains negative, meaning the model cannot predict exact forward returns — though directional accuracy (92%) and trade-level profitability remain robust. Addressing this through expanding the out-of-sample window as live trading data accumulates.
Drawdown Management — Structural vs. Tactical
Max drawdown of -43.7% remains the primary area for improvement (vs. B&H -81.5%). Analysis of all DD > -30% events reveals they concentrate in STABLE regime periods where the model maintains 52% average exposure. A pre-crisis early warning system (VIX crossing 23 from below, BTC below 200-day MA with 90-day DD exceeding 15%) and STABLE-specific stop losses (-15% from entry after 30 days) have been implemented, reducing worst-case trade losses. However, sudden BLACK_SWAN events (VIX jumping from 17→37 intraday) remain structurally unhedgeable without options or futures overlays — an active development priority.
On-Chain Data Coverage
Primary on-chain data (MVRV, NUPL, SOPR) from BGeometrics covers 2022-present. Historical MVRV and NUPL are backfilled from CoinMetrics community data (2017-2022) using MVRV ratio and the mathematical identity NUPL = 1 - 1/MVRV. Full Glassnode Pro access would improve historical calibration with native Z-score computation. As the ForeDex report highlights, post-ETF on-chain metrics are structurally weakening — the framework already mitigates this by weighting macro factors more heavily in STABLE/TIGHTENING regimes.
Structural Long Bias
BTC appreciated 12x during the test period. Any long-biased strategy benefits from this tailwind. Short-side returns (TIGHTENING regime) are modest and less validated. The framework's real test will come in a prolonged sideways or structurally declining market.

07 Development Roadmap

Near-term: Probability-based regime scoring (continuous net score replacing discrete regime labels), reducing position whipsaw at regime boundaries. STABLE regime stop-loss calibration to reduce DD without sacrificing recovery participation. The immediate development targets are Sharpe ratio above 1.0, max drawdown below 30%, and CAGR above 20% — achievable primarily through improved drawdown management while preserving the core long conviction structure. The structural long bias inherent in the current model must be continuously monitored and adjusted as BTC matures as an asset class.

Medium-term: Alt-coin rotation layer using BTC dominance as a timing signal. ETF flow integration as a leading indicator for institutional positioning. DeFi yield/TVL metrics for on-chain liquidity assessment.

Long-term: Walk-forward validation framework with rolling 6-month OOS windows. Cross-asset macro regime model incorporating rates, credit, and commodities. Options overlay for tail risk hedging during BLACK_SWAN transitions.

08 Background

This framework was built by a commodity trader, not a quant. Over 12 years trading crude oil, naphtha, and petrochemicals across physical and derivatives markets at Samsung C&T and TRIPTIK TRADING SA (Geneva) taught me that markets are regime-dependent — the same price signal means different things in a contango vs. backwardation environment. The benzene/naphtha spread behaves differently when Chinese inventory is at 200K tons vs. 30K tons. You can't trade a single number; you need to know which regime you're in.

Crypto is no different. MVRV at 3.0 in an EXPANSION regime (M2 growing, DXY falling) is a hold signal. MVRV at 3.0 in a TIGHTENING regime is an exit signal. The framework encodes this context-dependency explicitly, which is why complexity in signal generation hurt performance but complexity in regime classification improved it.

The transition from discretionary to systematic trading is not about replacing intuition with algorithms. It's about encoding intuition into a repeatable, testable framework — and then being honest about where the framework breaks down.
