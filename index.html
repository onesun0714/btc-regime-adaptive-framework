CORE THESIS

Where does true alpha come from in a cycle asset?

Most BTC alpha models reduce, on inspection, to variations of long bias. They refine entry timing or add exit signals while preserving directional exposure. This research begins from a different question.

CORE QUESTION
In Bitcoin as a cycle-driven asset, once mechanical beta exposure is stripped away, where does genuine risk-adjusted alpha originate? And can it be statistically distinguished from chance?

Eight years of data analysis points to two distinct sources. First, regime-conditional position sizing during liquidity transitions. Second, phase distinction within seemingly homogeneous regimes — the EXPANSION regime separates into EARLY (signal unconfirmed) and MATURE (signal confirmed) phases with materially different forward risk-adjusted returns.

CORE DISCOVERY

EXPANSION phase separation: EARLY vs MATURE

Earlier model iterations treated EXPANSION as a single regime. Closer examination reveals two distinct patterns within what appears to be one regime.

EARLY phase: When liquidity expansion signals first appear (prior 60 days in different regime), macro signals remain unconfirmed. Capital flow lags. False signals are not uncommon. Examples: early 2018, late 2022.

MATURE phase: When EXPANSION has persisted >60 days. Macro signals confirmed, capital flows materialized, trend reliability substantially higher. Examples: late 2020, late 2024. Within MATURE EXPANSION, V18 incorporates the Short-Term Holder (STH) Realized Price ratio as a position sizing overlay. The signal — current price divided by the average cost basis of holders active in the past 155 days — captures momentum state independent of the composite score (partial IC +0.228 vs MVRV Z-Score, p<0.05), improving backtest Sharpe by +0.061 over the 2022–2026 test period (bootstrap p=0.875).

Fed Net Liquidity (Federal Reserve balance sheet minus Treasury General Account minus overnight reverse repo) was also tested as a supplementary liquidity proxy. Lead-lag IC analysis across 2- to 12-week horizons produced no statistically significant improvement, consistent with the existing macro axes already absorbing the relevant liquidity information.

STATISTICAL VALIDATION
90-day forward Sharpe: EARLY 0.35, MATURE 2.12. t-statistic 4.74, p-value <0.0001. Within identical score bands (50-65), EARLY vs MATURE return differential is +32 percentage points — confirming phase information adds value beyond score alone.

Bootstrap test: 95.7th percentile vs random

The strategy's actual positions were randomly reordered in time and the resulting backtest run 5,000 times. If timing is meaningless, the actual result should fall near the center of the random distribution.

HONEST LIMITATIONS

What this work does not claim

Sample concentration: 13 cycle trades over 8 years

13 complete long position cycles (with 117 portfolio adjustment actions). Top 3 trades account for 94% of winning P&L. This is below the typical 100+ trades benchmark for systematic strategies. However, it reflects Bitcoin's four-year halving cycle structure — meaningful trend opportunities limited to 1-2 per cycle. Inflating trade frequency would constitute over-trading and erode alpha.

Marginal Jensen's alpha

Daily regression alpha: +13.9% annualized, t-stat 1.83, p=0.067 (marginally significant). Conventional alpha frameworks struggle with high-noise daily data. Bootstrap (95.7%ile) and risk-adjusted metrics (Sharpe 0.88, Calmar 0.50) are more appropriate evaluation tools.

200-day MA crossover comparison

A simple 200-day MA crossover produces +31.9% CAGR, Sharpe 0.86, MaxDD -63.6%. Comparable risk-adjusted return. The strategy's edge over simple trend-following lies in:

  • 5pp drawdown improvement compounds with leverage (7.5pp at 1.5x)
  • Signal attribution — every trade traceable to specific triggering signals
  • Forward robustness — multi-signal architecture more adaptive to post-ETF regime change

Production infrastructure

This is research output, not production system. Not yet built: live execution infrastructure, realistic slippage modeling, capital capacity analysis, portfolio risk overlay, drawdown protocol, real-time monitoring, and forward live tracking record.
